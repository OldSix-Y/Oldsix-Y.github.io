Weakly Solvated Functional Additive Design for Sodium Battery Electrolytes
Master's Research Project (Lead Researcher) | 2023-2025
  • Designed a novel bi-functional additive to optimize solvation structure and stabilize interfacial chemistry in sodium metal batteries.
  • Performed DFT (Gaussian) and MD (MS, GROMACS, VMD) simulations to pre-screen additive molecules and elucidate solvation and SEI formation mechanisms, guiding experimental formulation.
  • Investigated ion transport kinetics by variable-temperature EIS and EIS-DRT, clarifying the evolution of electrolyte kinetics under the influence of additives on rate and cycling performance through insightful DRT analysis.
  • Verified the additive's effectiveness through electrochemical measurements, spectroscopic analyses, and interfacial characterizations, demonstrating improved ion transport and stable SEI formation.
  • Achieved >80% capacity retention after 2000 cycles at 10C and stable cycling over 2500 cycles at 2C with negligible capacity fading.
Outcome: Manuscript submitted to Angewandte Chemie International Edition (Under Review).
