Machine Learning-Assisted High-Throughput Screening of Electrolytes for Batteries

Lead Researcher (2025-Now)

Developed a machine learning-assisted strategy to accelerate electrolyte discovery, addressing the limitations of conventional trial-and-error approaches with high cost and unpredictability. Generated a diverse library of CHOF-containing molecules using the Sauge isomer generator and custom Python scripts. Performed DFT calculations (Gaussian) and MD simulations (GROMACS) to extract key physico-chemical properties for database construction and model optimization. Conducted high-throughput screening based on viscosity and ion-solvent interaction strength to identify promising electrolyte candidates, with experimental validation of electrochemical performance.

GitHub Repository

Weakly Solvated Functional Additive Design for Sodium Battery Electrolytes

Master's Research Project (Lead Researcher, 2023-2025)

Angewandte Chemie International Edition (Under Review)

Designed a novel bi-functional additive to optimize solvation structure and stabilize interfacial chemistry in sodium metal batteries. Performed DFT (Gaussian) and MD (MS, GROMACS, VMD) simulations to pre-screen additive molecules and elucidate solvation and SEI formation mechanisms, guiding experimental formulation. Investigated ion transport kinetics by variable-temperature EIS and EIS-DRT, clarifying the evolution of electrolyte kinetics under the influence of additives on rate and cycling performance through insightful DRT analysis. Verified the additive's effectiveness through electrochemical measurements, spectroscopic analyses, and interfacial characterizations, demonstrating improved ion transport and stable SEI formation. Achieved >80% capacity retention after 2000 cycles at 10C and stable cycling over 2500 cycles at 2C with negligible capacity fading.

Design of Non-Flammable Electrolytes for High-Safety, Long-Life Sodium Batteries

Lead Researcher (2024-2025)

Developed flame-retardant and functional co-solvents to address flammability and safety issues in sodium batteries while maintaining high electrochemical performance. Combined MD simulations (GROMACS) and NMR spectroscopy to reveal an intermediate solvation structure distinct from conventional AGG configurations. Established a solvation design strategy that balances safety with electrochemical efficiency, achieving over 4000 stable cycles and excellent flame-retardant behavior.
